About

Kate is currently enrolled in the two-year bookbinding program at the North Bennet Street School in Boston. At NBSS, she is learning historical and contemporary bookbinding techniques, as well as basic book and paper conservation under the instruction of Jeff Altepeter and Martha Kearsley. She is currently the Program Co-Chair for the New England chapter of the Guild of Book Workers and a member of the Guild of Book Workers. She would be happy to discuss any binding, box making, or repair projects you might have.

Kate grew up in Albuquerque, NM and attended Wesleyan University in Connecticut, where she graduated in 2009. She moved to the Boston area just after school and worked and interned at various independent book publishers for six years. She lives in Somerville, MA with her husband, Evan, and their hound, Thelma.

Diploma Cases

This was an edition of 120 full cloth diploma cases made for the graduation of the class of 2016 from the North Bennet Street School. Each case was stamped with two custom dies.

History of Saco and Biddeford

This book was rebound in an historically accurate binding. The original covers of the 1830 book and spine piece (which was added later on) were removed and the spine was cleaned. The boards were remade as paste boards and the spine piece was made from a starched linen. New endpapers were sewn on to the text block and the book was rebound using an adhered-boards binding. The boards were then sided up with a replica paper of the sugar paper used on the original boards. A half-leather box was built to house the rebound book.See the condition and treatment report here.

In Evolution's Attic

This was an edition of 50 letterpress printed books bound as a flat back in full cloth with a custom stamp on the front of the case. While the edition was completed as a class project, I oversaw the project and managed the tasks and the class in order to meet the client’s deadline.
